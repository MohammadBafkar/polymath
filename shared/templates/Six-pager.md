# {{title}}

> Amazon-style narrative. Prose paragraphs, not bullet slides. Target
> ~1,800 words (≈ 6 pages). The reader is silent for the first 15–20
> minutes of the meeting while they read.

## Introduction

One or two paragraphs. State the problem and why it matters now. End with
the question the rest of the document will answer.

## Tenets

3–5 short sentences naming what we will and will NOT trade off. Tenets are
load-bearing for the rest of the doc — every later choice can be argued
against the tenets.

- We will prioritize <X> over <Y>.
- We will not <Z> in order to preserve <W>.
- …

## Current state

What is true today, with evidence. Numbers, screenshots, customer quotes —
not assertions. This section is the bar everything else has to clear.

## Why now

The trigger. A deadline, a market shift, a regulatory change, a metric
inflecting. Without "why now", the doc reads as ambient anxiety; with it,
the urgency is shared.

## Approach

The narrative. Walk through the proposed approach in prose, not bullets.
Each paragraph should make exactly one point and be challengeable in the
appendix's FAQ.

## Risks and mitigations

Honest. The single most likely failure mode, the single most damaging
failure mode, and the mitigation for each.

## Success measure

How we will know in 30 / 90 / 180 days whether this worked. One metric per
window; a single observable measurement, not a basket.

## What we need to decide today

The specific ask of the room. "Approve the direction"; "fund the team";
"agree on the deadline". Not "share thoughts".

## Appendix: FAQ

The hardest 5–8 questions the doc must answer, with one-paragraph answers.
This is where the reader's objections go. Quality of the FAQ is the
quality of the doc.

## References

- Data sources, customer quotes, prior docs.
