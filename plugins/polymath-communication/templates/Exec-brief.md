# {{title}}

> BLUF: Bottom Line Up Front. One paragraph at the top with the decision,
> ask, or recommendation. Everything after is supporting detail.

## TL;DR

One paragraph. State the conclusion / ask / decision first. If the reader
stops here, they should still walk away with the headline.

## What's happening

Two paragraphs max. Quote the concrete signal that motivated this brief —
a metric, a customer report, a deadline, a constraint. No background unless
it's load-bearing for the ask.

## What we're recommending

One option, named. Not three options for the exec to pick from — that's
their job, but you're the one closest to the work.

## Why this and not the alternatives

Two short bullets. Name the next-best alternative and the trade-off you'd
accept.

- Alternative: …  Trade-off: ….
- Alternative: …  Trade-off: ….

## What we need from you

The specific ask. Decision required by <date>? Approval to spend <$>?
A sign-off on direction? Be unambiguous about what "yes" means.

## What happens if we say yes

Two bullets max. Timeline, owner, observable outcome.

## What happens if we say no

One bullet. The cost of inaction. Honest, not hyperbolic.

- Underlying doc / PRD / ADR / data.
