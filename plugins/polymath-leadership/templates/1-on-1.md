> The 1:1 is **the direct report's meeting**. The manager prepares; the
> direct report drives. Skip-cancellations corrode the relationship; default
> to keeping it.

## Manager's agenda (when needed)

- Career / growth.
- Feedback (in both directions).
- Strategic context (what's coming, why it matters).
- Project-specific blockers I can unblock.

## Pulse check

One question rotated weekly:

- "What's the most energizing thing you worked on this week?"
- "What's draining you right now?"
- "How are you, the human, doing?"
- "What would you change about how we operate?"
- "Where are you stuck that you haven't asked for help on?"

## Skip protocol

If we have to skip:

- 1st skip: reschedule within the week.
- 2nd skip: red flag. Manager owns rescheduling and apologizes.
- Cancel for "no agenda" is forbidden. There's always an agenda — see Pulse.
